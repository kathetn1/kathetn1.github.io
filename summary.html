INF 133 - Summary

User Research

User Interviews

In the user interviews, I used the standard questions that were given as default. From the interviews, I learned about what my peers were expecting and looking for in a web portfolio. I also learned about their personal experiences with creating their own web portfolios and their job experience. Overall, everyone has a similar goal of finding a stable job after college or gaining more experience in the field.

Competitor Analysis

I thought all of the sites were unique and I wish I had previous experience to make my site seem as fluid and creative as theirs. They all had their own unique styles and different ways to present their information.

Feature Value Matrix

I chose these features based on what I thought users would be looking for in a portfolio. I ranked them based on that as well.

Feature Prioritization

Based on my thoughts from the Feature Value Matrix, I graphed it accordingly and kept those points in mind while creating my site. Not all of this worked and I had to make some compromises for the sake of time.

User Testing

User Stories

The user stories were helpful because it allowed me to focus more on what users might actually be looking for or looking at when they view the site.

Cognitive Walkthroughs

Cognitive Walkthroughs helped me understand how people actually really used my site. What they liked, what they disliked, what was easy to find, what was more difficult.

Click Test - Helped me get a quantitative value on what people would try to click in order to find resume or skill set. Question Test - Similarly helped me get feedback on the level of difficulty to navigate the site.

Summary of Findings

After the User Testing portion, some of the feedback I received:

  • Change the color scheme
  • Too many images
  • Information is not easy to find

Incorporation of Findings

Some of the features I changed:

  • Color Scheme
  • Layout
  • Fonts
  • Added Menu Button

A lot of the changes I implemented were things I knew I wanted to do after I had submitted the midterm project, but I didn't have enough time to figure out how to do everything I wanted.
